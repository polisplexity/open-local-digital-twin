# Do No Harm

Open Local Digital Twin is built for public-interest city intelligence. It must
not be used to make unsupported claims about people, communities, damage,
vulnerability, safety, or public-service priorities.

## Design Rules

1. Open data is evidence, not automatic truth.
2. City-authoritative decisions must be explicit and auditable.
3. The system should preserve source, license, method, quality, and provenance.
4. Public outputs should not expose personal microdata.
5. Inferred layers must be labelled as inferred until validated.
6. Operational claims require authority-grade data or expert review.
7. Private provider layers must remain separate from public baselines unless
   intentionally accepted for publication.

## AI And Semantic Packs

Semantic packs and AI-assisted workflows should explain:

- what inputs they use;
- what rules they apply;
- what they can claim;
- what they cannot claim;
- what a city or analyst must verify next.

The platform should not present inferred outputs as official city decisions.

## Sensitive Use Cases

Extra review is required for deployments involving:

- emergency response;
- policing or surveillance;
- migration or vulnerable populations;
- health data;
- conflict or reconstruction contexts;
- school, child, or household-level data;
- predictive prioritization of services or benefits.

## Human Review

Open Local Digital Twin is a decision-support and evidence-management runtime.
It is not a replacement for public authority, expert judgement, community
consultation, or legal review.
