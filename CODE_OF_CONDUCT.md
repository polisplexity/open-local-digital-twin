# Code Of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Open Local Digital Twin is a public-interest technology project. We expect
contributors, users, reviewers, maintainers, and partner organizations to work
with respect, technical honesty, and care for communities affected by civic-data
systems.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Explain technical claims with evidence.
- Respect privacy, safety, and local context.
- Keep public-data, provider-data, and authority-grade claims clearly separate.
- Give appropriate attribution to data sources, standards, and contributors.
- Report security or privacy issues responsibly.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing personal, private, or sensitive data without a lawful basis.
- Sharing credentials, tokens, private deployment details, or internal partner
  material.
- Presenting inferred, incomplete, or experimental outputs as official public
  authority decisions.
- Using the project to target vulnerable communities, surveil individuals, or
  bypass legitimate public-interest review.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, reject issues or pull requests, block accounts,
or restrict participation when behavior creates safety, privacy, legal, or
community risk.

Security and privacy concerns should be reported through `SECURITY.md`.
